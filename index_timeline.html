Freshman Year

Getting Started! Be familiar and get comfortable with your school

You're probably gonna be spending the next four years here so take some time to learn the layout of the school.

Starting Out on the Right Foot Meeting your Guidance Counselor

Introduce yourself to your guidance counselor within the first few months of school to build a solid relationship with your counselor.

Your guidance counselor will send a letter of recommendation to all colleges you apply to. Talking with your guidance counselor now will allow him to write a fantastic and unique recommendation letter.

Branching Out Get Involved in Extracurriculars!

Chances are high school offers a lot more clubs than middle school so try some out. Never programmed? Try out the programming club for a bit.

Try out many extracurriculars to find something you're passionate about whether it be art, math, or debate. Even try out a new sport. Being on a sports team shows your ability to collaborate.

Colleges love seeing extracurriculars; it adds an extra layer of depth to academics.

Setting Up for The Future Develop Good Study Habits

Learning good, reliable study habits now is best thing to do freshman year. This year will be the easiest so it is the best time to learn what works for you.

Reach out to older siblings, upperclassmen, teachers, or see our study habits page for help on building good study habits.

Sophmore Year

Overall Advice Keep going strong!

  1. Stick with 1 or 2 clubs.
    • Colleges love consistency. Long-term involvement is preferable to involvement in many clubs.
  2. Get a leadership position in any of your clubs.
  3. Keep in touch with your guidance counselor
    • The more they get to know you, the more personalized your recommendation will be.
  4. Volunteer consistently and for something pertaining to your interests
    • Colleges like seeing community involvement
    • Volunteer over the weekend. If you don't have time in the school year, volunteer over the summer.
  5. Choosing the Right Classes
    • Make sure to choose challenging courses like honors or AP
    • Showing a gradual increase in course rigor reflects well on you as colleges can see that you can handle the workload
    • However, be sure to not take on too many hard courses otherwise your GPA will drop
    • Maintain a fine balance between course rigor and solid GPA

Fall Starting Standardized Testing

  • October:
    • The PSAT/NMSQT Oct 14th or Oct 28th
    • There is no need to prep for this test but you should still take it
    • The PSAT/NMSQT gives an idea of your strengths and weaknesses.
    • Taking this test allows Khan Academy to create a personalized study plan
  • Follow the Overall Advice outlined above

Winter Keep Going!

  • Find a summer internship
  • Study for AP Exams!!

Spring More Testing

  • February-March: PSAT 10, the new PSAT, opens
    • PSAT 10 is only open to 10th graders
    • There is no advantage to taking this test over the PSAT/NMSQT
    • Study for AP Exams
  • May: AP Exams
    • Make sure you are prepared to take the AP Exams!
    • AP Testing Schedule
  • May/June: SAT Subject Tests
    • Take the corresponding SAT Subject test for the AP classes you took
  • Continue to follow the overall advice

Summer Looking Forward to Junior Year

  • June-August: SAT Prep vs ACT Prep
    • Choose which test you want to take
    • If you have trouble choosing, take a blind practice test and choose which one you prefer
    • Find a prep course either online or in person
  • June: Teacher Recommendations
    • Think of 2 teachers you want to ask for recommendations
    • These teachers should be ones that know you very well and can write a detailed letter tailored to you

Junior Year

Overall Advice Keep Calm

Junior year is the critical year of high school. Doing well is very important because many colleges focus on this year for GPA and extracurriculars.

  1. Keep your GPA up and if possible show an upward trend
  2. Stay on top of your AP courses!
    • Studying for the AP test well in advance will make life much easier
  3. Stay strong with your extracurriculars and get leadership positions
    • Leadership roles show strength of character
  4. A month before you take either the ACT/ SAT, take 1 or 2 practice tests each weekend before taking the real deal
  5. Foster a good relationship with some teachers for recommendation letters

Fall Get Ready!

  • September:
    • Touch base with your guidance counselor again - fill him in with things you've done over the summer
    • Think about which teachers you want to ask for recommendations
    • ACT (Sept 12th)
  • October:
    • Take the SAT (Oct 3rd) / ACT (Oct 24th). This gives you plenty of time to retake the test if you don't meet your goals
    • Try your best on the PSAT/NMSQT even though it doesn't affect college admission
      • Doing well on the PSAT/NMSQT can earn you a National Merit Scholarship
  • November:
    • Think about which schools you want to apply to
    • Research schools that interest you - find out their pros and cons
    • Keep up your GPA, volunteer, and do extracurriculars
    • If you didn't get the score you want, take the SAT again (Nov 7th)

Winter Almost Halfway!

  • December:
    • Ideally, you want to take the SAT/ACT for the last time in Dec. This allow you more flexibility in schedule.
    • SAT (Dec 5th) and ACT (Dec 12th)
    • Keep following the Overall Advice stated above
    • Over winter break, visit some schools nearby that you are interested in
    • While visiting schools make sure to take notes and pay attention. This will help you decided which school to enroll in.
  • Janurary:
    • Retake SAT(Jan 23rd) if needed - No ACT offered
    • Look into summer internships/programs at nearby colleges
    • Continue to follow the overall advice
    • Keep on studying for AP tests
    • Research more schools and if you want to visit them plan that out for Feburary Break
    • Halfway There!
  • Feburary:
    • Continue to study for AP tests
    • Take the ACT(Feb 6th) if needed - No SAT offered
    • Visit schools during Feburary Break
    • Start considering which teachers you want to write you a recommendation letter
    • Find out about summer intership/volunteer opportunities

Spring
Study, Study, Study!

This might be the most intense time for standardized tests so be ready!

  • March:
    • Study for AP Tests! Getting great scores on AP tests looks awesome and can get you out of intro level courses in colleges so don't slack off!
    • Find/Apply for summer internship programs - make your summer productive!
    • Don't neglect your extracurriculars and try to get leadership positions in your clubs
    • Take the SAT(Mar 5th) if needed - No ACT offered
  • April:
    • Study for AP Tests!
    • Keep up GPA and extracurriculars
    • Take the ACT(April 9th) if needed - No SAT offered
    • Consider which SAT Subject Tests you want to take - take the corresponding SAT Subject Tests for whatever AP course you are taking
    • If you are unsure which Subject test to take, ask your guidance counselor or AP/ honors level teacher, he/ she can tell you if you are prepared to take the appropriate Subject Test
    • Take a practice Subject Test to see if you are ready to take it on May 7th
  • May:
    • Study for AP Tests!!
    • Know the AP Testing Schedule
    • If you feel prepared, take the corresponding Subject test on May 7th - take only 1/2 tests
    • Make sure you have a plan for the summer besides Netflix and food

Summer The Finish Line!

  • June:
    • Finish up the Subject Tests or retake the SAT(Jun 4th) or take the ACT(Jun 11th)
    • Ask 2 teachers to write your recommendation letter
    • Continue to research potential schools and visit them
  • July/August:
    • Over the summer, think about essay ideas
    • Do some introspection
  • August:
    • Common App opens August 1st
    • Nail down a list of schools you want to apply to
    • If time allows, visit more schools or do intensive research

Senior Year

Overall Advice Avoid Senioritis

  1. Stay Strong!
    • Keep your grades respectable even after you have been accepted into a college
    • Don't neglect leadership opportunities
  2. Maintain Involvement in Extracurriculars
  3. Don't forget to have fun!

Fall Crunch Time

This time of year is stressful so be prepared and start early!

  • August:
    • Make a Common App account within the first week of Aug
    • Begin writing your Common App essay - give to friends/ family to revise/ edit
    • Sit down with your parents and discuss your financial situation
    • Start constructing a list of schools you want to apply Early Decision, Early Action, and Regular Decision to
    • Look into Scholarships
    • Brainstorm essay ideas for the supplements essays specific to each school
    • Consider buying/checking out books with examples of well written college admission essays
  • Septmeber:
    • Talk with you guidance counselor and fill him in with what you did over the summer
    • Meet with the teachers who will write your recommendation letters and verify in they can do - make sure they know when they need to write your letter
    • Have a mostly concrete list of schools ready
    • Continue working on your application essays
  • October:
    • Polish those essays!
    • Make sure your letters of recommendation are ready
    • Know what schools you want to apply early to
    • Research the financial aid deadlines for each of the schools you are applying to
  • November:
    • Send in your ED/EA application on Nov 1st
    • Continue working on RD essays
    • Research as many scholarship opporunities as possible

Winter A Light at the End of the Tunnel?

  • December:
    • Most colleges notify applicants of their acceptance/ deferral/ rejection by mid December so check your mail
    • If you are turned down, focus on your RD schools
    • Over the winter break, allow yourself some time to recuperate before writing more supplemental essays
    • Apply to scholarships
  • Janurary:
    • Start getting ready for RD applications
    • RD deadline ranges from Jan 1st - Feb 1st so be on top of due dates
    • If you have been accepted, keep your grades respectable because colleges can request to see your midterm grades
    • Submit the FAFSA by Jan 1st if needed
    • Apply to scholarships
  • Feburary
    • Continue working on RD application
    • Don't forget to send a thank you card to teachers who wrote you an recommendation letter
    • Take some time to relax and enjoy your senior year

Spring College Enrollment

  • March:
    • RD responses should come in by mid/late March
    • Enjoy senior year!
  • April:
    • Decide what school you want to attend
  • May:
    • May 1st is national college decision day so you must decide which college to enroll in

Summer The Start of a New Journey

  • June:
    • Enjoy graduation!
    • Spend time with friends and family
    • Work on that bucket list
